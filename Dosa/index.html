Delicious Dosa Recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup rice
  2. 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  3. 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  4. Salt to taste
  5. Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately in water. Soak them along with fenugreek seeds in enough water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and urad dal. Grind them separately to a smooth batter using a wet grinder or blender, adding water as needed.
  3. Combine both batters in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well. Cover and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until the batter doubles in size.
  4. Once fermented, stir the batter gently. Heat a non-stick skillet or tawa over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the skillet and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
  6. Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crispy. Flip if desired and cook for another minute.
  7. Remove from the skillet and serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Serving Suggestions

Dosa is best enjoyed with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and a bowl of hot sambar. You can also add a filling of spiced mashed potatoes to make Masala Dosa.


Idli Recipe

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup rice
  2. 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  3. 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  4. Salt to taste
  5. Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice and urad dal separately in water. Soak them along with fenugreek seeds in enough water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and urad dal. Grind them separately to a smooth batter using a wet grinder or blender, adding water as needed.
  3. Combine both batters in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well. Cover and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until the batter doubles in size.
  4. Once fermented, stir the batter gently. Grease idli molds with oil or ghee and fill them with the batter.
  5. Cover the molds and steam for 10-15 minutes until the idlis are cooked through.

Serving Suggestions

Idlis are best served hot with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and sambar. You can also sprinkle some ghee on top for added flavor.
